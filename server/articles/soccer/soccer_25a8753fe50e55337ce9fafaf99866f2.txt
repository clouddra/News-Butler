Dennis Diekmeier has signed a new deal at Hamburg following lengthy contract negotiations.The right-back's previous agreement was due to expire at the end of this season, and in September the club admitted they feared losing the 23-year-old when an agreement appeared to be some way off.However the former Nurnberg player has now put pen to paper on the new deal for the club who currently lie in 14th place in the Bundesliga."We are happy to have agreed a new contract with Dennis Diekmeier," said Hamburg general manager Oliver Kreuzer."We have always said we would like to keep on with him at our club. Dennis is an important player in our squad, he has made great progress and is still just 24."Diekmeier added, "We have great quality in the squad and I would like to contribute to the future success of Hamburg."
