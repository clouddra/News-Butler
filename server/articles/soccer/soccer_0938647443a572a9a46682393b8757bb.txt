Four Serie A clubs have been handed suspended stadium bans following crowd incidents over the weekend.AC Milan, Inter Milan, Roma and Torino were all found guilty after discriminatory chants were heard in the matches between Roma and Napoli, Milan and Udinese, and Torino and Inter, the league confirmed.The four clubs were ordered to close a section of their stadiums, while Milan, Inter and Roma were fined 50,000 euros, with all the sentences suspended for one year.
